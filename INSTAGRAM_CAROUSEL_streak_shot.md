SLIDE 2 — THE CONCEPT
Headline: REAR CURTAIN SYNC
Body: Flash fires at the END of exposure.
Not the start.
The bike draws a blur trail FIRST — then the flash freezes it sharp.
The streak is behind the bike. The motion makes sense.

---

SLIDE 3 — THE GEAR
Headline: WHAT YOU NEED
• Canon R6 II (any mirrorless works)
• 28–70mm f/2.8 @ 35–40mm
• Godox X3 Pro trigger — Rear Curtain mode
• Flash A: V860III nearside / fill
• Flash B: AD200 farside / rim light
• Heavy tripod. Non-negotiable.

---

SLIDE 4 — THE SETTINGS
Headline: THE NUMBERS
Mode: Manual
Shutter: 0.5s – 0.8s
Aperture: f/8 – f/11
ISO: 100
Sync: REAR CURTAIN
Flash A: 1/16 – 1/8 power
Flash B: 1/4 – 1/2 power

SLIDE 6 — THE WINDOW
Headline: THE BLUE HOUR WINDOW
Body: 20 minutes after sunset.
Not during. Not 40 min after.
The sky is deep blue. The road is dark.
The flashes do all the work.
You get ~15 frames. Make them count.

---

SLIDE 7 — THE RIDER BRIEF (solo note)
Headline: SHOOTING YOUR OWN BIKE
Body: Solo?
Timer delay + remote trigger.
Bike in 1st gear at idle on a slight incline.
Mark the freeze point. Trust the pre-focus.
The bike doesn't need a rider to make the shot.

---

SLIDE 8 — TROUBLESHOOT
Headline: WHEN IT GOES WRONG
Too much blur → shutter to 0.4s
No rim light → increase AD200 power
Streak in front of bike → you're on front curtain — check your trigger
Bike too dark → V860III up 1 stop
